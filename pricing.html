Competitive Analysis

A teardown is the act of disassembling a product such that it helps to identify its component parts, chip and system functionalities, and component costing information. Results are disseminated through photographs, component lists, assembly and mechanisms descriptions. Disassembly may be destructive. Three main purposes of performing teardowns and comparing teardowns are to 1) identify innovations to improve a product in its design, functionalities and engineering / manufacturing, 2) estimate costing of a bill of materials (BOM) to find improvements in costs and 3) if necessary, be used in court and litigation proceedings to show where intellectual property or patents might be infringed by another firm’s part or product.

Dextrous Design Engineering performs teardown analysis starting at $1500 and increases in rate with added complexity of a product, which requires additional analysis time. When multiple product teardowns are requested, we include a comparative analysis at the conclusion free of charge.
